💡 April Wisdom Tip

The different types of love

📋 Quick Steps:
  • Agape: The primary love expressed in the Bible. Love without condition, selfless, sacrifcing
  • Eros: Passionate love, particularly in spousal relationships.
  • Philla: Friendship, brotherly love or based on mutual understanding.
  • Storge: Based on affection between family members
  • Pragma: Pratical love, where there is an understanding, in a committed relationship.
  • Philautia: Self love, healthy, not about vanity but the ability to love yourself in such a way that you can extend it to others.

Love Never Fails

📖 📖 1 Corinthians 13: 8-13
Abilities may cease but love never fails, it will last forever. Spiritual maturity requires us to act in love, to develop our ability to love and our interest in loving and sharing with others. We are encouraged to use love to take us through this time of limited understanding.
💭 Key Points:
  • Love is Enduring(verses 8-10)
    Your history affects your response, scripture informs us and brings correction. Abilities may cease but love never fails; it's an Enduring trait
  • Love is a mature spiritual disposition (verse 11)
    Spiritual maturity requires us to act in love, we are to develop our ability to love, our interest in loving and sharing love with others.
  • Love is the greatest principle available to us (verses 12-13)
    Re-reading and meditating on scripture with the guidance of the Holy Spirit helps, we are encouraged to use love to take us through this time of limited understanding.
▶ 📅 March 8th

The Lamentations of Jesus

📖 📖 Matthew 26: 36-46
Searching the scripture to find out how Jesus handled some of the same struggles we experience in this life
💭 Key Points:
  • Matthew 26:36-46:
    In the Garden of Gethsemane, Jesus pleaded to the Father to be delivered from death, while seeking the Father's will instead of His own. He accepted and trusted the Father's decision.
  • John 11: 33-35
    Jesus lamented at the death of Lazarus because He saw and felt the pain His creation was experiencing
  • Luke 19:41-44
    Jesus wept over Jerusalem because of its future destruction and the eventual fate of those who rejected Him.
  • Matthew 27-46
    Jesus cried out on the cross,`My God, why have you forsaken me`.
  • Applicaitions:
    Face hard realities
    Lament (feel and express the pain)
    Pray, trust and move forward

▶ 📅 March 22nd

Jesus's plan to evangelize

📖 📖 John 4:7-13
Jesus spoke with the woman at the well, despite her being a Samaritan, who the Jews did not associate with, she was most likely an outcast because of the time she was at the well. He told her that He is the Messiah, the one they were looking for, she believed, shared what he told her with her fellow villagers and they believed because of her testimony.
💭 Key Points:
  • Jesus brought the message to the woman at the well.
  • He got her attention by reaching out to her.
  • He demonstrated that salvation is for everyone.
  • That true salvation recognizes sin.
  • That He is the Messiah, the one to bring salvation.
  • She believed and testified to the others.
  • They believed because of her testimony.

Featured Testimony

❝
At the age of 12 years, I gave my heart to the Lord Jesus Christ, my Savior. One evening many years ago, I saw some people setting up mics, speakers and equipment opposite where I used to live in East Dry River (called Harlem). They were members of the St Anns Evangelical Bible Church and they were having an outreach program or street meeting, so I sat on the porch and listened. Afterwards they came to my gate and asked if they can have a discussion with my family, they then presented the good news of Jesus Christ. On that night I asked Jesus into my heart and boy did my life change! I was overwhelmed with joy knowing that I had eternal life. The next day at school, my friends asked if I was going crazy, they saw a change in me, I was overjoyed. I soon followed in water Baptism at the Victory Heights Bible Camp. So I want to leave a little encouragement to the readers: 'Give Jesus Christ a chance'.
— Brother Dexter Kanhai
